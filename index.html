Aerospace Engineer at the University of Maryland, College Park (UMD)

About Me

My current work revolves around mechanical design, manufacturing, and structural analysis. For the summer, I am working at BAE Systems as a systems engineer for Trident Submarine operations. I am a proud lead for an all-undergraduate engineering team called UMD Loop and a researcher for the Composites Research (CORE) Laboratory at the University of Maryland.

Competing with UMD Loop

Since my Freshman year, I have been a core member of UMD Loop engineering team, working to be the best collegiate team and inspire more engineers to join our team and learn technical skills. From building tunnel boring machines to now designing martian rovers, my experiences at UMD Loop have shaped my skill set and aspirations as an aerospace engineer.

Researching at the CORE Lab

The Composites Research (CORE) Laboratory looks to advance smart structures and composites in the field of robotics. My personal research centers around pneumatic artificial muscles, which is a type of composite actuator that outputs an axial and radial force after being inflated. I design testing machines and try to characterize the strength of these muscles.

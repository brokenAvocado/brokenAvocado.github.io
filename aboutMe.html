About Me

Education

I am an Aerospace Engineering major with a minor in Robotics, and I am a student at the University of Maryland, College Park. Initially, I was a mechanical engineering major, but after joining UMD Loop and being enthralled by the space industry and spacecraft, I could not resist switching majors.

Why Engineering

Ever since playing with Legos as a child, I was born to do hands-on work. Whether it is working outside or tinkering with something in the garage, I always love working with physical objects and seeing how assemblies come together. I was always fascinted with real life structures and mechanisms, wondering why designers would add a specific feature here or chose a specific material. This would be especially motivational when I started learning about structural mechanics.

This coincided with my competitive and perfectionist nature. I joined engineering teams all throughout highschool and into college. Not only did I love my work with physically building and designing, but I enjoyed the challenge and the grind the finish assembling robots or tunnel boring machines together. Spending late nights at the garage was a stressful experience, but the catharsis from finally finishing projects and the excitement of competing was really a joy.

Explore through my experiences of being part of a competition team, research group, and many more.
